Global Engineering Milestones

First Powered Flight by the Wright Brothers (1903)

Country: United States

Orville and Wilbur Wright achieved the first powered, sustained, and controlled airplane flight, pioneering the field of aerospace engineering.

First patent for color TV (1940)

Country: Mexico

Guillermo González Camarena patented a chromoscopic adapter with which black and white cameras of the day could capture color.

First Programmable Computer - Z3 (1941)

Country: Germany

The world's first programmable, fully automatic computer and a pioneer for future computing technologies.

Akashi Kaikyō Bridge (1998)

Country: Japan

Also known as the Pearl Bridge, it's the world's longest suspension bridge with a span of 1,991 meters and is an engineering marvel of resilience and design.

Large Hadron Collider- LHC (Operational since 2008)

Country: International (CERN located in Switzerland and France)

The world's largest and most powerful particle collider, built by the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN), represents a milestone in mechanical and electrical engineering.

Three Gorges Dam (2008)

Country: China

It was designed not only to generate electricity to propel China’s breakneck economic growth, but also to tame China’s longest river, shield millions of people from fatal floods and, as a symbol of technological prowess in hydraulic engineering.

Falcon 1 First Privately Developed Liquid Fuel Rocket to Orbit (2008)

Country: United States

Developed by SpaceX, the Falcon 1 was the first privately developed liquid fuel rocket to reach orbit, marking a new era in private spaceflight.

Burj Khalifa (2010)

Country: United Arab Emirates

Standing at over 828 meters, the Burj Khalifa is the tallest structure in the world, representing a monumental achievement in civil engineering.

First Wrireless Radio

Country: Italy

Guglielmo Marconi built the equipment and transmitted electrical signals through the air from one end of his house to the other, and then from the house to the garden.

Google News

Country: United States & India

At Google, Mountain View,Krishna Bharat led a team developing Google News, a service that automatically indexes over 25,000 news websites in more than 35 languages to provide a summary of the News resources.
